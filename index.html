Capital Allocation Analysis

ConnectGreaterWashington Investment Decision

Overview

This analysis compares three development sites: Poplar Point, Project Connect, and Tysons Central, using financial feasibility, sensitivity testing, and policy leverage.

While Project Connect and Tysons Central strengthen existing growth corridors, Poplar Point represents a different type of investment focused on waterfront activation and long-term civic value.

Based on the analysis, Sanctuary at Poplar Point is selected as the preferred investment site.

Sensitivity Analysis

Real estate feasibility is highly sensitive to changes in rent, construction costs, and cap rates.

Risk Interpretation

  • Project Connect: Maintains positive performance under all stress scenarios due to strong institutional and transit anchoring.
  • Tysons Central: Shows significant decline under cap rate and cost stress due to office market exposure.
  • Poplar Point: Negative stress outcomes reflect early-stage redevelopment conditions, not weak demand.

Poplar Point’s sensitivity reflects high upfront activation costs required to unlock long-term waterfront value.

Policy Leverage Comparison

Policy leverage reflects how effectively each site converts public investment into long-term public value.

  • Poplar Point: Highest policy leverage. A waterfront transformation opportunity that advances equity, public health, and civic access in an underinvested area.
  • Project Connect: Moderate policy leverage. Supports innovation clustering and transit-oriented development but reinforces an existing growth corridor.
  • Tysons Central: Lower policy leverage. Incremental suburban redevelopment with limited civic transformation.

Poplar Point expands opportunity rather than reinforcing existing patterns of growth.

Final Recommendation

Based on the combined financial and policy analysis, Sanctuary at Poplar Point is selected for immediate advancement.

While it has the lowest near-term financial return, it delivers the highest long-term public value through waterfront activation, civic infrastructure, and catalytic development potential.

Project Connect and Tysons Central remain strong but primarily optimize existing systems rather than creating new urban value.
